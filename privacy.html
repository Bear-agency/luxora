PRIVACY POLICY

Luxora Finance Ltd · Last updated: April 7, 2026

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy (the “Policy”) describes how Luxora Finance Ltd (“Company”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, processes, discloses, and protects personal information in connection with the provision of its financial services.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring compliance with applicable Canadian privacy laws, including the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), as well as anti-money laundering and regulatory obligations.

By using our services, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Policy.

2. Scope of Policy

This Policy applies to:

  • All customers and users of our services
  • Visitors to our website and platforms
  • Business partners and counterparties

This Policy applies regardless of how personal information is collected (online, offline, or through third parties).

3. Definitions

“Personal Information” means any information that identifies an individual

“Processing” means collection, use, storage, disclosure, or destruction

“Services” refers to all financial services provided by the Company

We collect personal information necessary to provide services and comply with legal obligations.

4.3 Financial Information

  • Transaction history

4.4 Technical Data

5. How We Collect Information

We collect data:

  • Directly from you
  • Through our platform
  • From third-party verification providers
  • From public databases and regulators

We process personal information for the following purposes:

  • Identity verification (KYC)
  • Processing transactions
  • Fraud prevention and risk management
  • Compliance with legal obligations
  • Improving our services
  • Customer support

7. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your data based on:

We retain personal information:

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures:

12. Your Rights

12.1 Right of Access You have the right to request confirmation as to whether we process your personal information and, where that is the case, to request access to such information. This includes the right to obtain details regarding the categories of data processed, the purposes of processing, recipients or categories of recipients, and the expected retention period. Upon request, we shall provide a copy of your personal data in a commonly used electronic format, subject to applicable legal restrictions.

12.2 Right to Rectification You have the right to request the correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated personal information. We will take reasonable steps to ensure that any inaccuracies are corrected without undue delay.

12.3 Right to Withdraw Consent Where processing is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw such consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent shall not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to such withdrawal. Please note that withdrawal of consent may limit or prevent your ability to use certain services.

12.4 Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten) You may request deletion of your personal information where:

  • the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected;
  • you withdraw consent and there is no other legal basis for processing;
  • the data has been processed unlawfully.

However, this right is subject to legal obligations, including retention requirements under applicable financial and anti-money laundering laws, which may require us to retain certain data.

12.5 Right to Restriction of Processing You have the right to request that we limit the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, including where you contest the accuracy of the data or object to processing. During such restriction, we may continue to store the data but will not process it further except where legally required.

12.6 Right to Data Portability Where technically feasible and applicable, you have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and to request transmission of such data to another service provider.

12.7 Right to Object You have the right to object to processing of your personal information where such processing is based on legitimate interests, including profiling. Upon receiving such an objection, we will assess whether compelling legitimate grounds exist to continue processing.

12.8 Right Related to Automated Decision-Making You have the right to request information about the logic involved in automated decision-making processes, including profiling, and the potential consequences of such processing. You may also request human intervention where decisions significantly affect you.

12.9 Right to Lodge a Complaint You have the right to file a complaint with the relevant data protection authority if you believe your personal information has been processed in violation of applicable laws. In Canada, this includes the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada.

12.10 Limitations of Rights Certain rights described in this section may be limited or restricted where required by law, including obligations related to anti-money laundering, fraud prevention, legal claims, and regulatory compliance.

12.11 Exercising Your Rights To exercise your rights, you may contact us using the contact details provided in this Policy. We may require you to verify your identity before processing your request. Requests shall be processed within a reasonable timeframe and in accordance with applicable legal requirements.
